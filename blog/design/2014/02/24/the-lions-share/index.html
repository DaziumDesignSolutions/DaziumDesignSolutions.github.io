The Lion’s Share

It’s an expression that means “the largest portion”. For us at Dazium, the idiom is especially apropos as we recently analyzed our Facebook stats from this past year, 2013. Who has the largest portion of likes aka followers in our domain? What platforms are they using to share their content? What is it they’re doing that has users liking them, responding to their tweets and even reading their blogs? That got us thinking. If we’re putting this much time into our advertising efforts, certainly our clients are doing the same. And if not, then it’s time to help them develop a content sharing strategy for 2014.

What’s your content sharing strategy?

Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, Blogger, LinkedIn are just a few of the well-known social networking sites that have surfaced on the web since the early 2000s. The social media craze has propelled companies to establish and maintain external micro-sites. Managing that content and keeping it current takes time, but what makes it move is a well-thought out and executed content sharing strategy. So, if you don’t have one in place (even if you do) start by following these steps:

  1. Pick your social media platforms carefully
    Start small. It’s important to recognize that managing multiple social media platforms requires manpower and time. These social media platforms topped the list for businesses in 2013: Facebook, Google Plus, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, Pinterest, foursquare.
  2. Share content multiple times/places
    At Dazium, we have our Facebook posts linked to our website so they’re visible from our website on our homepage. That’s just one example of sharing content. You may decide to use tools that allow you to post the same content at different times depending on your follower’s time zones.
  3. Repopulate/Reuse good content
    Good content demands to be seen and shared multiple times. Some concepts need to be repeated. We often revisit design style guidelines as the good ones never die.
  4. Encourage people to share your content/posts via social sharing plugins & click to Tweet button.

Having a well-executed content strategy just may make the difference in who get’s the Lion’s Share.
