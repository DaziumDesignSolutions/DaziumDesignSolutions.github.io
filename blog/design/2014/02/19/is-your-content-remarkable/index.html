Is Your Content Remarkable?

“Even when you are marketing to your entire audience or customer base, you are still simply speaking to a single human at any given time. Worry less about sounding professional and worry more about creating remarkable content that other humans can relate to.” - Ann Handley, Chief Content Officer at MarketingProfs

It’s a hefty job designing content that users can relate to, react to, even move them to act. And in 2014 the platforms are even more diverse – print, web, mobile, video. Creating remarkable content that other humans can relate to may seem like a ‘pie in the sky’ effort. But it doesn’t have to be. It does take work though, like staying abreast of current content trends and understanding user behavior.

2014 Content Design Trends

What’s at the helm for companies today is advertising via social networks. Companies jumped onboard with Facebook, Twitter and even LinkedIn, and as the research on these sites becomes more tangible, there is a strong pull to further develop and cement their presence in these fields. We have even helped clients design content for their Wikipedia page.

But regardless of the platform (print, web, social media sites, mobile, apps, etc.) you’re designing for, keep content:

  • Scannable - Short bursts of content. Call outs such as headlines, bolded copy, graphic containers, and images.
  • Relevant - how is your product or service essential to current & potential customers? Appeal to their emotion.
  • Simple – keep to an 8th grade reading level:
    • Don’t use complicated sentence structures.
    • Don’t use long words.
    • Don’t use a lot of technical jargon.
    • Use common everyday words.

Consistency Is Key

While cutting copy you designed for one platform and pasting it verbatim into another won’t work, repopulating content is necessary in order to keep the message consistent. Designing content today requires knowledge of the platforms where your content will reside. Understanding how content will be shared (capabilities & restrictions) is just as important as what the message is. Users interact with and approach each communication platform differently – depending on their purpose, uses and interest.

Content is powerful and has been a driving factor behind marketing communication strategies. But what gives content its power is the user.

User Behaviors

Users approach and interact with content in varying ways. Understanding your audience will help you design to their behavior. Users can be categorized as:

  1. Skimmers – also known as ‘taskers’. These users have shorter attention spans. The will weed out the “fluff” and zero in on the information that strikes them as relevant and valuable.
  2. Emoters – users will react to what appeals to their emotions either positively or negatively.
  3. KISSers – Keep it simple - the majority of users can easily read and comprehend an 8th-grade level. Simplified language opens your content to a much larger audience.
  4. Information Junkies – users who will read front to cover. The ones who click on the read more buttons every time.
