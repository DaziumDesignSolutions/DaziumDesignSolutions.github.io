Pay Attention

Yes, pay attention to all the graphic design you are surrounded with daily, from advertising to product packaging, from web to billboards, you are surrounded by it. What gets your attention, what is captivating? What designs to you respect? Amongst writers, marketers and designers a lot of time is allocated to answer those questions. Everyone wants their product to get your attention.

One challenge as a designer is to work with content so it improves readability. This applies to both extremes: projects with more content than will ideally fit as well as projects where more white space is allowed. Both require a different direction, a different way of considering options and designing to accommodate.

For the client, most of the time they are only thinking about what needs to be said. As designers it is our job to arrange that content, organize it effectively, and make recommendations to the client. We also need to and add photos and illustrations that relate to the content and help the reader understand. Good, readability design does all these things.

Improving Readability

Attention span is very minimal for most people, you want your designs to stand out. Every audience is different, but there are a few objective design tips and tricks that apply to most every product.

  1. Before designing, scan the content and make quick decisions about how the content will be read and what items are the most important.
  2. Identify what you want the reader to see first, second, third, forthâ€¦ and so on
  3. Determine what design style you are going to match for your concept. Either the client has a brand you are matching or you are building the style based on the audience.
  4. Organize your content by sections and give white space or graphic space between each of the sections. Use font size, color, or spacing to give a title more importance to sections.
  5. Start working with the least important sections first, and make sure that content is working at a readable font size. Next work your way up to the most important title or image that you want to be seen first.
  6. Try multiple layout options, sometimes what you start with isn't what ended up working. As you work with the content you start understanding what works best.
