Hey, Look Here!

Did that get your attention? Or was it the graphic next to it? From titles to the images that accompany the content, designers design for attention. They want to know what their audience finds captivating and which designs they respect. A design can make or break a marketing message. It takes careful planning to get it right.

Take content design for example. A designer must often work with content so it improves readability of the message. This applies to projects where there is more content than will ideally fit as well as to projects where more white space is allowed. Both require a different direction, a different way of considering options and designing to maximize the message.

As designers, good readability design includes arranging the content, organizing it effectively, adding photos and illustrations that relate to the content when necessary so that the audience can understand the message.

Improving Readability

Attention span is very minimal for most people – so how do you make your design stand out especially when every audience is different? I’ve compiled a few objective design tips and tricks you can use that apply to most every product.

  1. Before designing, scan the content and make quick decisions about how the content will be read and what items are the most important.
  2. Identify what you want the reader to see first, second, third, forth... and so on
  3. Determine what design style you are going to match for your concept. Either the client has a brand you are matching or you are building the style based on the audience.
  4. Organize your content by sections and give white space or graphic space between each of the sections. Use font size, color, or spacing to give a title more importance to sections.
  5. Start working with the least important sections first, and make sure that content is working at a readable font size. Next, work your way up to the most important title or image that you want to be seen first.
  6. Try multiple layouts, sometimes what you start with isn't what ended up working. As you work with the content you start understanding what works best.
