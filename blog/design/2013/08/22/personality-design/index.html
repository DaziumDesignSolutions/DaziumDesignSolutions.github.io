‘Cause you got Personality

Brand, Personality
Design, Personality
Marketing Message, Personality

Lloyd Price sang it about a girl, but in today’s design world, it could easily relate to a company’s brand identity aka personality. Every company has one, and the designer’s job is to bring that personality to life. If the company doesn't have any previous designs to match, or they are searching for a new brand, graphic designers will start with the personality of the company with an eye on the end goal – the best way to communicate with the audience and stand out from the competition.

So, you got personality.

As the client, you first need to have an idea of the specifications or concepts that fit the personality of your company in order for the designer to bring it to life. Start with these key considerations before initiating a design and engaging a designer:

About Usage

Who is your target audience and what is appropriate? How will the company use this new “marketing tool”? When is this project due? Will you need support/service when completed? What is your strategy to re-evaluate the success of this marketing tool?

About Design:

What is the end deliverable (e.g. web, email, print)?
What is the company's project goal?
Does it follow the creative brief and marketing approach?
What is your budget?
How much design is appropriate?
What is the shelf life of the “tool”?
Is this a one-time project or on-going image development?

Graphic design is key to communicating a company's brand aka personality

Any designer will be able to get you off the ground and running and a good designer will ask questions and build a story about what the company needs are based on budget and goals. Designers have the unique ability to organize an emotion, a personality into an image. That image or brand has many tasks, but first and foremost, it has to provide a good user experience. While a brand personality can offer many different interpretations, there are a few important points that need to be conveyed in any design.

Honesty and Authenticity

With all the spam people see every second, designers are getting better at picking out the marketing messages that the audience really wants to see. Don't try to market something that you are not, it will always turn against you.

Uniqueness and Exclusivity

As a company what makes you unique, how do you stand out? The design has to reflect your differentiator and reveal why a user would choose your company over another. A good design will give the user the answer as quickly as possible.

Confidence

The best marketing materials are positive and self-assured. They bring people together and will bring new clients to your company.
