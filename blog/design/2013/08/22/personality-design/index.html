Design with Personality

Graphic design is always a company's marketing message, and when completed you will own the design. In most cases designers are working either to match existing branding or creating a new brand for clients. If the company doesn't have any previous designs to match, or they are searching for a new brand, graphic designers will start with the personality of the company with an eye on the end goal – the best way to communicate with the audience.

Gotta Start Somewhere

To start, have an idea of the specifications or concepts that fit the personality of the company. It is always good to see examples of what you like and expect. Next, a good designer will ask questions and build a story about what the company needs based on budget and goals. Every company has a personality, a designers job is to bring that identity to life with a new style and designs that really stand out from the competition.

Questions for any company to review before starting a design is a good idea to make sure the designer knows where to start.

Usage:

  • What is your target audience and what is appropriate?
  • How will the company use this new “marketing tool”?
  • When is this project due?
  • Will you need support/service when completed?
  • What is your strategy to re-evaluate the success of this marketing tool?

Design:

  • What is the end deliverable (e.g. web, email, print)?
  • What are the company's project goal?
  • Does it follow the creative brief and marketing approach?
  • What is your budget?
  • How much design is appropriate?
  • What is the shelf-life of the “tool”?
  • Is this a one-time project or on-going image development?

Visual Language

After all these questions are answered, any designer will be able to get you off the ground and running. Designers have the unique ability to organize an emotion into an image. That image or brand has many tasks, but first and foremost, it has to give user experience. While a brand personality can offer many different interpretations, there are a few important points that need to be conveyed in any design.

Honest and Authentic
With all the spam people see every second, we are getting better at picking out the marketing messages that the audience really wants to see. Don't try to market something that you are not, it will always turn against you.

Unique and Exclusive
As a company what makes you unique, how do you stand out. The design has to reflect your differentiator and why. Why would a user choose your company over another. A good design will give the user the answer as quickly as possible

Confident
The best marketing materials are positive and self-assured. These are great qualities anyone wants to be around. They bring people together and will bring new clients to your company.
