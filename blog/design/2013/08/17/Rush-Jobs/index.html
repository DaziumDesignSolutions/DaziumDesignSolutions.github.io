Rush Jobs

So, you have a quick turn-around and you want to deliver a well-designed product – what do you do? What you don’t do is panic.

Graphic design is sometimes a high-expectation, quick deadline service and it can be tough producing when even the smallest detail of a spelling issue will make or break a project. As the designer, meeting ever increasing tight deadlines means both taking responsibility and setting client expectation. It also means having the gumption, self-direction and creativity to make decisions, define the problem for the client and solve it.

As with most things, success starts with good communication. It is time management, communication and always understanding the final product. You have to consider the client is probably a bit worried about getting the project finished. As the designer, you have to be able to communicate what is reasonable to expect and what concessions have to be made to make the deadline. This skill can feel like a constant learning curve. But, when everything is in balance, rush jobs can be fun. The quick back and forth approvals are instant, with no lag time between tasks, you don't have to set aside and then relearn the project details to pick up again, and it builds an enormous amount of trust.

At the core, graphic design is for the client and not the designer. It has to match the client's needs for both the individual and the collective company. If you solve a problem like a rush job for a client they will give you good referrals and use you for future work!
